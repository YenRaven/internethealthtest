Why do these tests matter?

Disputes between Internet service providers have degraded the consumer broadband across the United States, as companies withhold normal upgrades in order to charge unnecessary tolls. The result is that access to popular content slows down for everyone. Running this test provides data on the harm from these practices through collecting information about potential slowdowns on your connection.

How does the test work?

The Internet Health Test runs a few short speed tests to several measurement servers on different networks to compare how your broadband connection performance varies to different parts of the Internet. This information collected from users in the same region can begin to identify cases where business disputes are interfering with broadband across. These tests shouldn't take too long and do not collect personal information from your browser.
